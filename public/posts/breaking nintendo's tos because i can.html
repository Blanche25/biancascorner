ok so basically i've had a nintendo switch since basically its launch, but after the first 3ish years i just kinda stopped using it.

why did i decide to stop using 300+ euros worth of console? well, my non-existant reader, the reason is simple: joycon drift was driving me absolutely insane. it was terrible. i even tried buying a brand new set of joycons some time after, but can you guess what happened to those? can you guess??????? yeah they started to drift too, even faster than the first pair.
so here i am, 7 (i think?) years later, suddenly wanting to make it usable, since there's some games i'd like to play on it (persona 5 royal my beloved (yes i could just play it on pc i just think the switch is way more convenient))

so i started looking around for fixes for the joycons, and i'd heard that nintendo was offering free repairs for joycon drift. so. i sent it to them... and they asked for 60 euros, which is insane cuz that's literally the price of a new pair of joycons so i gave up on that.

until about a couple months later when i discovered that the issue just fixes itself by shoving a literal piece of cardboard inbetween the stick and the battery for some arcane reason?? but that's nice ig.

but then... i discovered... to play games... i have to... buy them... *dies*
which is why i decided to give modding my switch a try
ok piracy isn't the only reason, i also wanted to theme my homescreen since i saw some people do it and i thought it was really cool.
also i had already modded my 3ds some time before that so really how hard could it possibly be?

well to be fair i genuinely thought it was gonna be a lot harder but all in all it was pretty chill! most of the time it took was spent just waiting for files to transfer to my sd card.
also for anyone interested this is the guide i used, very easy to follow and explains all the steps well enough that even a dunce like me can do it.

so yeah! it works pretty well, it's a bit annoying having to make sure it never turns off completely (otherwise you have to re-inject the payload which ig is fine since you can do it by connecting your switch to your phone with rekado) and setting up things is a bit harder since there's a lot less documentations than what i'm used to with the 3ds, but in the end i think it was definitely worth it!

right now i'm playing p5r and sea of stars, another rpg which so far i think is pretty cool. i might make a section of the site just to talk about games/media in general... or maybe i'll just use the blog to talk about them. who knows!
